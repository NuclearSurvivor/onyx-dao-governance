**For purposes of ALL proposals herein:**

### 1. ENTITY DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Community Member"** means any wallet holding XCN tokens on Ethereum, BNB Chain, BASE, or Goliath (upon launch).

**1.2 "Active Community Member"** means a Community Member who has demonstrated verifiable participation within the past 90 days through: governance voting, testnet activity, Zealy engagement (minimum 100 XP), or documented community contributions.

**1.3 "Insider"** means any individual or entity that: (a) receives compensation from the DAO exceeding $10,000 annually, (b) holds a formal DAO role (leadership, ambassador, service provider), or (c) has treasury access or MultiSig authority.

**1.4 "Coordinated Group"** means wallets that have transferred XCN between each other, share common funding sources, or exhibit voting patterns with >95% correlation over 10+ proposals.

**1.5 "Service Provider"** means any entity receiving DAO funds in exchange for services, including but not limited to: Chain, development contractors, marketing firms, auditors, and infrastructure providers.

**1.6 "Leadership"** means individuals holding the following roles: Head of DAO, Head of Business Development, Community Manager, Ambassador Leader, or any role with treasury access or MultiSig authority.

### 2. GOVERNANCE DEFINITIONS

**2.1 "Governance Vote"** means a vote cast through the official Onyx governance mechanism (currently polls.onyx.org and on-chain governance).

**2.2 "Simple Majority"** means approval by more than 50% of votes cast.

**2.3 "Supermajority"** means approval by 60% or more of votes cast, unless otherwise specified.

**2.4 "Strong Supermajority"** means approval by 66.67% (2/3) or more of votes cast.

**2.5 "Constitutional Majority"** means approval by 75% (3/4) or more of votes cast.

**2.6 "Quorum"** means the minimum participation required for a vote to be valid, as defined per proposal type.

**2.7 "Business Day"** means Monday through Friday, excluding internationally recognized holidays.

### 3. FINANCIAL DEFINITIONS

**3.1 "Treasury"** means all XCN and other assets held by DAO-controlled wallets.

**3.2 "Allocation"** means any transfer of treasury funds to external parties.

**3.3 "Grant"** means a one-time allocation for a specific purpose.

**3.4 "Recurring Allocation"** means an ongoing periodic allocation (monthly, quarterly, etc.).

**3.5 "Large Allocation"** means any single allocation exceeding 50,000,000 XCN.

**3.6 "Critical Allocation"** means any single allocation exceeding 500,000,000 XCN.

### 4. PROGRAM DEFINITIONS

**4.1 "Testnet"** means the Goliath testnet environment for pre-mainnet testing.

**4.2 "Mainnet"** means the production Goliath blockchain network.

**4.3 "Testnet Points"** means points earned through testnet participation, calculated at 1 XCN staked/held = 1 Point.

**4.4 "Validator"** means an entity operating node infrastructure to validate transactions on Goliath.

## POLL 1.1: Codify 1,000,000 XCN Governance Proposal Threshold

### Status Note
The governance system currently operates with a 1,000,000 XCN proposal threshold. This poll formally codifies that standard into governance rules to prevent it from being raised without community consent.

### Decentralization Rationale
A governance system where 99.92% of token holders cannot create proposals is not decentralized — it is oligarchic. The 1,000,000 XCN threshold represents the minimum viable barrier against spam while allowing ~730 community wallets to propose, versus only ~50 at the former 10,000,000 threshold. This directly distributes power over the governance agenda from a tiny insider class to a broad community base. Formalizing this threshold through governance ensures it cannot be silently raised back by leadership without explicit community approval — protecting the inclusive governance that decentralization requires. This mirrors the approach of Compound, Uniswap, and Aave, where proposal thresholds are locked by governance, not unilaterally set by teams.

### Proposed Change
Formally codify the 1,000,000 XCN governance proposal threshold in the governance ruleset. Any increase to this threshold requires a Supermajority community vote (60%+) with 14-day notice period.

At 1,000,000 XCN:
- Approximately 730 community wallets can create proposals
- Represents broad community access while preventing spam
- Threshold cannot be raised by leadership without community approval

### Benefits
1. Protects Inclusive Governance — prevents backsliding to oligarchic threshold
2. Diverse Perspectives — 14x more voices in governance agenda
3. Reduced Insider Control — insiders cannot unilaterally gatekeep proposals
4. Alignment with DAO Principles — governance power flows from community participation

### Implementation
Record current 1M threshold as formal governance rule. Effective immediately upon passage.

### Vote Options
- **For** — Formally codify 1,000,000 XCN threshold, require Supermajority to change
- **Against** — Allow threshold to be changed without community vote

## POLL 1.2: Establish On-Chain Accountability Standards for Leadership

### Decentralization Rationale
True decentralization means accountability is enforced by cryptographic systems and transparent on-chain records — not by exposing personal identities. Requiring real names and KYC of DAO participants contradicts the foundational principles of decentralized governance, exposes individuals to personal liability and physical harm, creates a centralized identity registry (a single point of failure and attack), and was already rejected by this community (Poll #67: "Should multisig signers be publicly doxxed?" — FAILED). Bitcoin, Ethereum, MakerDAO, Compound, and virtually every major decentralized protocol operate with pseudonymous contributors. Accountability comes from transparent on-chain behavior, not from knowing someone's legal name. A multisig structure inherently prevents any single anonymous actor from unilaterally controlling funds — making personal identity disclosure redundant for security purposes.

### Summary
Establish enforceable on-chain accountability standards for all Leadership positions without requiring personal identity disclosure. Accountability is achieved through wallet transparency, immutable on-chain voting records, and smart-contract-enforced multi-party authorization.

### Current State
Leadership accountability is informal, inconsistently applied, and relies on social trust rather than verifiable on-chain commitments.

### Proposed Change
All Leadership positions must publicly disclose and maintain:
1. **Primary DAO Wallet Address** — publicly posted and immutably linked to their role on-chain
2. **On-Chain Activity Record** — all governance votes, treasury interactions, and role-related transactions publicly viewable
3. **Pseudonymous Professional Profile** — consistent pseudonymous identity with verifiable track record (Discord handle, forum history, governance participation log); real-world identity is NOT required
4. **Conflict-of-Interest Disclosures** — any financial relationships with service providers or competing protocols disclosed on-chain
5. **Multisig Enforcement** — no Leadership wallet may unilaterally authorize treasury transactions; all treasury actions require minimum 3-of-5 independent co-signatures

**Covered Positions:**
- Head of DAO
- Head of Business Development
- Community Manager
- Ambassador Leader
- Any role with treasury access or MultiSig authority

### What This Does NOT Require
- Real name or photograph
- Government ID or KYC
- LinkedIn or centralized social platform profile
- Any disclosure that could endanger individuals or expose them to legal harassment

### Implementation
- 30-day compliance period for wallet and profile disclosure
- Non-compliant individuals must step down or relinquish treasury access
- All disclosures recorded on-chain or in a publicly accessible immutable registry

### Vote Options
- **For** — Establish on-chain accountability standards for leadership (pseudonymous, no doxxing)
- **Against** — Maintain current informal accountability standards

## POLL 2.3: Proposal Resubmission Cooling Period

### Decentralization Rationale
Decentralized governance can only function if community votes are respected as binding expressions of the community's will. When rejected proposals are immediately resubmitted under a new name until they pass — exploiting whale participation windows — the governance process is being gamed, not used. Cooling periods protect the integrity of every vote by ensuring that the community's "no" is treated with the same weight as its "yes." This is not a restriction on governance — it is protection of governance. Without cooling periods, well-capitalized insiders can exhaust the community with repeated resubmissions until a favorable participation window is found, which is a form of governance capture incompatible with genuine decentralization.

### Summary
Implement cooling periods for rejected proposals.

### Current State
**Documented circumvention:**
- OIP-36: 80% AGAINST Layer 3 → OIP-54: 100% FOR (rebranded "XCN Ledger")
- OIP-51: 51% AGAINST Justin Sun → OIP-52: 98% FOR (same proposal)

### Proposed Change

| Rejection Margin | Cooling Period |
|------------------|----------------|
| >50% Against | 90 days |
| >66% Against | 180 days |
| >75% Against | 365 days |
| Failed quorum (>80% approval) | 30 days |
| Failed quorum (<80% approval) | 90 days |

**Requirements:**
- Resubmissions must state: "Modified version of [X] rejected on [date] with [X]% opposition"
- Renaming (e.g., "Onyxchain" → "XCN Ledger" → "Goliath") does NOT reset cooling period

### Vote Options
- **For** — Implement cooling periods
- **Against** — Allow unlimited resubmission

## POLL 2.11: Clawback Mechanism for Failed Deliverables

Decentralized governance can only commit treasury funds responsibly if it can also recover those funds when commitments are broken. Without clawback mechanisms, service providers face no consequences for non-delivery — creating a one-way door where community funds flow out without enforcement. Smart contract escrow with milestone-based release turns clawback into a trustless, automatic mechanism: no individual can arbitrarily decide whether to recover funds; the code enforces the community's terms. This is the decentralized standard for service provider accountability, replacing personal trust with programmable enforcement.

### Summary
Enable recovery of funds when deliverables are not met.

**Threshold:** All allocations >25M XCN must include:
- Specific deliverables
- Timeline for completion
- Success criteria (measurable)
- Clawback triggers

## POLL S.5: Standardize Live Onyx Points to 0.1 XCN Points per XCN per Day

### Decentralization Rationale
The current live Onyx Points system distributes a fixed shared pool (5 million points per day) proportionally among stakers. This creates a zero-sum dynamic where early participants' points are diluted as more stakers join — participants are competing against each other for a fixed allocation rather than earning based on their own contribution. A pooled distribution system obscures what a point is actually worth, makes it impossible for participants to calculate their rewards in advance, and creates incentive structures that favor large whales who capture a larger share of the fixed pool over consistent smaller participants. The 0.1 XCN Points per XCN per day standard eliminates this hidden complexity: your rewards are a direct, transparent function of your own stake, calculable by anyone. The testnet rate (Poll S.4) is set higher at 1:1 to specifically incentivize testnet participation — the live rate of 0.1 reflects the different purpose of the mainnet program while preserving the same fairness principle: no pooling, no dilution, no insider multipliers.

### Summary
Adopt a flat rate of **0.1 XCN Points per 1 XCN staked per day** for the live (Season 3+) Onyx Points program on both Ethereum and Onyx Ledger. Replaces the current fixed-pool proportional distribution with a transparent, individual-based earning rate.

### Current State (Season 2)

## POLL I.3: Treasury Diversification for Operational Runway

### Decentralization Rationale
A DAO treasury held entirely in its own native token is exposed to a catastrophic failure mode: if XCN price declines significantly, the DAO may lack the fiat-equivalent funds to pay for critical services (audits, infrastructure, development) even if it holds nominal billions in XCN. Protocol death by illiquidity — not by governance failure — has ended multiple decentralized projects. Quarterly conversion of a small treasury percentage to stable assets via OTC trades (not open market selling, which suppresses price) provides 12 months of guaranteed operational runway regardless of market conditions. This protects the community's ability to continue operating the protocol through bear markets, which is the foundation for long-term decentralized governance to function at all.

### Summary
Authorize quarterly conversion of up to 2% of the treasury to stable assets via OTC trades, protecting 12 months of minimum operational runway against sustained bear market conditions.

### Structure
- **Maximum per quarter**: 2% of total treasury value
- **Method**: OTC trades only (no open market selling)
- **Target stable assets**: USDC, USDT, or DAI
- **Runway target**: Minimum 12 months of operational costs in stable assets

### Operational Cost Definition
Minimum operational costs include: smart contract infrastructure, security audits, bug bounty funding, and minimum community management. Does NOT include discretionary spending (marketing, partnerships, ambassador programs).

### Triggers and Limits
- Conversion only executes if runway reserve is below 12-month minimum
- Stops automatically once 12-month runway is reached
- Maximum cumulative stable holdings: 10% of total treasury value
- Any single OTC trade >50M XCN USD equivalent requires MultiSig approval + public disclosure within 24 hours

### Transparency
- Monthly disclosure of stable asset balance
- OTC counterparty and terms disclosed within 7 days of each trade
- Quarterly runway calculation published publicly

### Vote Options
- **For** — Authorize quarterly treasury diversification up to 2% via OTC for operational runway
- **Against** — Maintain 100% XCN treasury
